Maekju

Maekju, the Korean term for beer, has a relatively recent but rapidly evolving history in Korea. Introduced during the late 19th century through interactions with Western cultures, beer gained popularity among the Korean population. Initially considered a foreign novelty, beer consumption increased significantly during the Japanese occupation (1910-1945) when breweries were established to cater to the demand. Post-Korean War (1950-1953) and with the influence of American culture, beer became a mainstream beverage, leading to the establishment and growth of Korean breweries. The 1960s saw the emergence of local beer brands, and by the late 20th century, beer became a ubiquitous part of Korean social life. The 21st century witnessed a craft beer renaissance, with a surge in microbreweries and a growing appreciation for diverse beer styles, transforming maekju from a foreign import to a dynamic and integral element of Korea's contemporary drinking culture.
