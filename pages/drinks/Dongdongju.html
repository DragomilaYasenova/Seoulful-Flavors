Dongdongju

Dongdongju, a traditional Korean rice wine, boasts a history deeply rooted in the country's cultural and agricultural heritage. Originating during the Goryeo Dynasty (918-1392), this unfiltered milky rice wine was a staple in Korean communities, particularly among farmers who brewed it using homegrown ingredients. Dongdongju gained prominence during festive occasions and celebrations. The brewing process involves fermenting a mixture of rice, water, and nuruk (fermentation starter) in onggi, traditional earthenware jars. The lack of filtration leaves the beverage with a slightly cloudy appearance, adding to its rustic charm. Dongdongju's popularity persisted through the Joseon Dynasty and beyond, becoming a symbol of Korean conviviality. In recent times, the resurgence of interest in traditional Korean beverages has revitalized Dongdongju, making it a sought-after delicacy both domestically and internationally, showcasing Korea's rich cultural tapestry through its flavors.
