Drinks
Soju (소주)

Soju is a popular Korean distilled alcoholic beverage known for its clear and neutral taste, often compared to vodka. Made primarily from rice, barley, or wheat, it typically has a lower alcohol content than traditional spirits, ranging from 16% to 25%. Soju is frequently consumed in social settings, where it is served in small glasses and enjoyed alongside various Korean dishes.

Bokbunja Ju (복분자주)

Bokbunja Ju is a Korean raspberry wine that stands out for its unique and robust flavor profile. Made from the Bokbunja raspberry, this wine offers a perfect blend of sweetness and tartness, creating a delightful and well-balanced taste. With its deep red color and distinctive fruity notes, Bokbunja Ju is a popular choice among wine enthusiasts and is often savored for its distinctive character.

Dongdongju (동동주)

Dongdongju is a traditional Korean cloudy rice wine celebrated for its distinctive appearance and unique taste. Characterized by its milky, unfiltered appearance, this fermented rice beverage maintains a pleasantly sweet and slightly tangy flavor profile. Often enjoyed in social gatherings and paired with Korean cuisine, Dongdongju's hazy quality adds to its charm and cultural significance in the realm of Korean traditional drinks.

Oksusu Cha (옥수수차)

Oksusu Cha, or Roasted Corn Tea, is a popular Korean beverage known for its toasty and comforting flavor. Made by roasting corn kernels, this tea offers a unique and nutty taste with a hint of sweetness. Enjoyed both hot and cold, Oksusu Cha is a versatile and soothing drink that has gained popularity for its distinctive aroma and healthful properties.

Green tea (녹차)

Green tea holds a cherished place in Korean culture, renowned for its health benefits and elegant simplicity. In Korea, green tea, or "Nokcha," is often enjoyed in various forms, including loose leaves, powdered matcha, or as part of traditional tea ceremonies. Known for its fresh, grassy flavor and antioxidant properties, Korean green tea reflects a rich cultural heritage and is a staple in both daily life and festive occasions.

Maekju (맥주)

Maekju, the Korean term for beer, is a widely enjoyed beverage in Korea, often consumed in social settings and paired with a variety of Korean dishes. Whether sipped in bustling city pubs or enjoyed during outdoor gatherings, maekju holds a significant place in Korean drinking culture. With a range of options from light lagers to craft brews, maekju contributes to the convivial atmosphere of Korean social life.

Boricha (보리차)

Boricha, a traditional Korean barley tea, is a beloved beverage made by steeping roasted barley in hot water. Renowned for its toasty aroma and mild, nutty flavor, Boricha is a popular choice for hydration throughout the year in Korea. Embraced for its caffeine-free properties, Boricha is often served chilled in summer or enjoyed hot in winter, making it a versatile and refreshing staple in Korean households.

Maesilju (매실주)

Maesilju, a traditional Korean plum wine, is celebrated for its sweet and tangy taste derived from ripe green plums. Produced through a meticulous fermentation process, Maesilju captures the essence of summer with its fruity and aromatic profile. Often enjoyed as a refreshing dessert wine or aperitif, this Korean specialty holds cultural significance and is cherished for its unique flavor in various social settings.
