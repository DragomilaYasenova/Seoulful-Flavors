Side dishes
Kimchi (김치)

Kimchi, a staple in Korean cuisine, is a spicy and fermented cabbage dish, showcasing the unique flavors of gochugaru and traditional fermentation. This iconic side dish reflects the heart of Korean culinary traditions, celebrated for its tangy and umami taste.

Danmuji (단무지)

Danmuji, a staple in Korean cuisine, refers to pickled radishes characterized by their vibrant yellow color and sweet-tangy flavor. Served as a popular side dish, these pickled radishes offer a refreshing crunch that complements the richness of many Korean meals. Danmuji's versatility and distinctive taste make it a beloved component of Korean dining experiences.

Kkakdugi (깍두기)

Kkakdugi, a traditional Korean dish, is a type of kimchi made with cubed radishes. These radish cubes are seasoned with a blend of spices, including garlic, ginger, and chili pepper flakes, creating a flavorful and slightly spicy profile. Kkakdugi's crunchy texture and bold taste make it a popular and distinctive side dish in Korean cuisine.

Ssamjang (쌈장)

PSsamjang, a popular Korean condiment, blends fermented soybean and chili paste for a savory kick. Commonly used in Korean barbecue, this versatile dipping sauce elevates the flavor of grilled meats and vegetables, enhancing the ssam dining experience.
