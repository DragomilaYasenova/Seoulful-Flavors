Naengmyeon

Naengmyeon, a Korean cold noodle dish, has its roots in the North Korean culinary tradition. Originating in the city of Pyongyang, naengmyeon gained popularity throughout Korea, especially in the summer months. Traditionally, it was served in a chilled broth with buckwheat noodles, julienned vegetables, and often a boiled egg and slices of meat. After the Korean War, North Korean refugees introduced naengmyeon to South Korea, where it underwent further adaptations. Two main varieties emerged: mul naengmyeon, served in a tangy iced broth, and bibim naengmyeon, dressed in a spicy, sweet gochujang-based sauce. Naengmyeon's popularity grew, and it became a staple in Korean cuisine. It's often associated with refreshing flavors and is enjoyed as a summertime delicacy. With its unique blend of textures and tastes, naengmyeon has become a beloved and iconic dish, showcasing the adaptability and diversity of Korean culinary traditions. A serving of naengmyeon can have approximately 300-500 calories.
